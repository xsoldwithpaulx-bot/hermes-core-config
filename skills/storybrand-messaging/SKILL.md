# storybrand-messaging

category: marketing
trust: local

## description

Applies StoryBrand-style messaging specifically for a Southern California real estate agent, positioning the homeowner as the hero and the agent as the guide. Used to rewrite website, emails, listing presentations, and ads around one clear narrative tied to the agent’s listing offer.

## instructions

When this skill is active, you:

- Assume you are writing for the same SoCal listing agent, markets (La Mirada / LA / Orange County), and core listing offer defined by the `hundred-million-offers` skill.
- Always structure messaging using the StoryBrand elements:
  1) Character: homeowner who wants to sell / move.
  2) Problem:
     - External: selling a home for max value with minimal stress.
     - Internal: fear of making a mistake, leaving money on the table, dealing with agents.
     - Philosophical: “You shouldn’t have to gamble with your biggest asset.”
  3) Guide: the agent (you) with empathy + authority.
  4) Plan: a simple 3-step plan tailored to your process.
  5) Call to action: direct (book a consult) and transitional (take the scorecard, get a free readiness review).
  6) Success: clear picture of life after a successful sale.
  7) Failure: what they avoid by working with you.

- For any request, output:
  - A brand script summary (bulleted SB7 elements).
  - The requested asset (homepage copy, one-liner, email, listing presentation section, ad, etc.) written in plain, conversational language.

- Always integrate:
  - Your Grand Slam listing offer.
  - Your scorecard/quiz as a gentle CTA where appropriate.
  - Local specificity (La Mirada / OC) instead of generic “your area.”

- Do NOT mention StoryBrand, frameworks, or SB7 by name in the final copy. The structure is invisible.

## examples

User: “Rewrite my homepage hero section for La Mirada sellers so it’s clear and compelling.”

Assistant:
- Produces:
  - One-liner.
  - Headline, subheadline.
  - 3-step plan.
  - Primary CTA (“Book your pricing & strategy session”).
  - Transitional CTA (“Take the 3-minute ‘Ready to Sell?’ scorecard.”)
